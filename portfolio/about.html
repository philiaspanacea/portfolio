About Me

I have always had a love for the arts and entertainment, and as a problem solver, I strive to find creative solutions that reflect who I am. This manifests in my love for technology as well, and I decided early on in my journey that I wanted to join my love for technology and art together. Design is the perfect marrying of the two and like Paul Rand says, there are so many different definitions, different manifestations of it. UI / UX design and graphic design are some of the paths I have followed in my design journey, and I am currently exploring video production and sound design.

There is always more room to grow and learn, and as I continue my journey in design, I hope to focus on expanding my knowledge in video production and sound design while still learning more about different fields of design. Alfred Hitchcock's quote resonates with me because as long as I can create something I can be proud of, I'll be happy. When I find myself in a rut, designing helps me relax and I often learn something new that inspires me and leads me into a rabbit hole.

Even outside of work, design is a large part of my life because of my love for the arts. I try to always learn something new, whether that's by watching a movie or reading a book about a topic out of my comfort zone, listening to analyses on principles of design, etc. because it's important to know about the possible new movements of art and the way they are affecting the climate. I think it's important to always be aware of what is going on in the world because it will always affect the art that is being created. When I want to tell a story through a design, "How do I want the viewer to feel" is an important question to ask because a design can carry different meanings depending on who sees it and when.
